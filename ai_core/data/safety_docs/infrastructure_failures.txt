Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. POWER OUTAGE

1.1 Immediate Actions:
- Emergency generators will activate automatically within 10-30 seconds
- Emergency lighting will illuminate hallways, stairwells, and exits
- Elevators will return to the ground floor and open doors automatically
- If you are in an elevator when power fails, remain calm — press the emergency button
- Do NOT use candles — use the flashlight on your phone or request one from staff

1.2 Guest Instructions:
- Remain in your room or current location until staff provides guidance
- Avoid opening refrigerators/minibars to preserve cold items
- Report any sparking outlets or burning smells immediately
- Unplug sensitive electronic devices to protect from power surge when power returns
- The front desk will communicate updates via door-to-door visits if PA system is down

1.3 Staff Response:
- Activate emergency operations plan
- Deploy staff to check all elevators for trapped guests
- Check on guests with medical equipment that requires electricity
- Monitor generator fuel levels
- Contact the power company for estimated restoration time
- Provide flashlights and candles (battery-operated only) to guests who request them

1.4 Extended Outage (over 2 hours):
- Offer meal service from available cold food items
- Arrange transportation for guests who wish to leave
- Provide regular updates (every 30 minutes) on restoration progress
- Consider relocating guests with medical needs to a facility with power
- Monitor building temperature (HVAC will be limited to generator capacity)

2. ELEVATOR MALFUNCTION / TRAPPED GUESTS

2.1 If You Are Trapped in an Elevator:
- Remain calm — you are safe, elevators are designed to be secure spaces
- Press the EMERGENCY button or ALARM bell
- Use the emergency phone in the elevator to call for help
- Do NOT try to force the doors open
- Do NOT try to climb out through the ceiling hatch
- Sit down if you feel dizzy or anxious
- Help will arrive within 15-30 minutes

2.2 Staff Response:
- Communicate with the trapped guests through the intercom or door
- Reassure them that help is on the way
- Call the elevator maintenance company immediately
- If guests have medical conditions, call emergency services
- Do NOT attempt to move the elevator manually unless trained
- Document the incident and follow up with affected guests

3. GAS LEAK

3.1 Signs of a Gas Leak:
- Smell of rotten eggs or sulfur
- Hissing or blowing sound near gas lines
- Dead or dying vegetation near gas lines (outdoor areas)
- Bubbles in standing water near gas lines
- Visible damage to gas connections

3.2 Immediate Actions:
- Do NOT turn on or off any electrical switches, lights, or appliances
- Do NOT use phones, lighters, or matches in the affected area
- Do NOT start any vehicles in the area
- Open windows and doors to ventilate if safe to do so
- Leave the area immediately
- Move at least 300 feet away from the suspected leak
- Call the gas company and 911 from a safe distance

3.3 Restaurant Kitchen Gas Leak:
- Turn off the gas supply at the main shut-off valve (if accessible and safe)
- Evacuate the kitchen and dining area
- Do NOT use kitchen exhaust fans (electrical risk)
- Ventilate by opening exterior doors
- Do NOT re-enter until the gas company gives the "All Clear"
- Chef/kitchen manager must account for all kitchen staff

4. WATER LEAK / FLOODING

4.1 Minor Water Leak (dripping pipe, toilet overflow):
- Place towels or buckets to contain the water
- Call maintenance immediately
- Turn off the water supply to the affected fixture if possible
- Move electronic devices and valuables away from the water
- If the leak is from above, move items away from the drip area

4.2 Major Water Leak / Pipe Burst:
- Call maintenance and the front desk immediately
- Turn off the main water supply to the area if trained to do so
- Turn off electrical power to the affected area (from the breaker panel, only if safe)
- Evacuate the affected area
- Move guests to alternative rooms
- Place "Wet Floor" signs to prevent slip hazards
- Document damage with photographs

4.3 Flooding from External Sources (Storm, Rising Water):
- Monitor weather alerts continuously
- Activate flood response plan if water approaches the property
- Move critical equipment and supplies to upper floors
- Sandbag vulnerable entry points
- Evacuate ground floor rooms if necessary
- Cut electrical power to flooded areas from the main panel
- Do NOT walk through flood water (electrical and contamination hazards)

5. HVAC (HEATING/COOLING) FAILURE

5.1 Air Conditioning Failure (Hot Weather):
- Open windows for ventilation if available
- Provide portable fans to guest rooms
- Offer cold water and ice to guests
- Move elderly and health-compromised guests to cooled areas
- Consider relocating guests if temperatures become dangerous (above 90°F/32°C indoors)
- Monitor guests for signs of heat exhaustion

5.2 Heating Failure (Cold Weather):
- Provide extra blankets to guest rooms
- Offer hot beverages in the lobby
- Move guests to rooms with working heaters or fireplace areas
- Consider relocating guests if temperatures drop below 55°F/13°C indoors
- Monitor guests for signs of hypothermia (especially elderly)
- Check pipe insulation to prevent freezing

6. INTERNET / COMMUNICATIONS FAILURE

- Notify guests of the outage
- Set up an information desk in the lobby for guest inquiries
- Use walkie-talkies for staff communication
- Maintain phone lines as backup (landlines often work during internet outages)
- Provide estimated restoration time from the service provider
- Offer alternative arrangements for business guests (nearby business center)

7. POOL EQUIPMENT FAILURE (RESORT-SPECIFIC)

7.1 Pool Pump/Filter Failure:
- Close the pool immediately
- Post "Pool Closed" signage
- Do NOT allow guests to swim in untreated water
- Call pool maintenance for emergency repair
- Test water chemistry before reopening
- Provide alternative activities for guests

7.2 Water Slide/Attraction Malfunction:
- Stop all rides immediately
- Clear all guests from the attraction
- Secure the area with barriers and signage
- Call the equipment manufacturer's emergency line
- Do NOT attempt repairs unless qualified
- Document the malfunction

8. STRUCTURAL DAMAGE

- If you notice cracks in walls, sagging ceilings, or buckling floors, report immediately
- Evacuate the affected area
- Do NOT enter rooms or areas with visible structural damage
- Call a structural engineer for assessment
- Cordon off the area with caution tape
- Relocate affected guests to alternative rooms or properties
