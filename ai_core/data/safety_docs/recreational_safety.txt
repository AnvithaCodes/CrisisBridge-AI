Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. SPA AND WELLNESS CENTER SAFETY

1.1 General Spa Rules:
- Inform your therapist of any medical conditions, allergies, or pregnancy
- Inform staff if you have high blood pressure, heart conditions, or diabetes
- Remove all jewelry before treatments
- Drink water before and after treatments to stay hydrated
- Shower before using shared facilities (sauna, steam room, pool)
- Do NOT shave or wax within 24 hours before body treatments

1.2 Sauna Safety:
- Maximum recommended time: 15-20 minutes per session
- Drink water before and during your sauna session
- Do NOT use if pregnant, intoxicated, or on blood pressure medication
- Exit immediately if you feel dizzy, nauseous, or have difficulty breathing
- Allow your body to cool gradually — do not jump into ice-cold water immediately
- Do NOT pour anything other than water on the sauna rocks
- Children under 12 are not permitted in the sauna

1.3 Steam Room Safety:
- Maximum recommended time: 15 minutes
- Do NOT use essential oils or other liquids without staff approval
- Exit immediately if you feel uncomfortable
- The floor is wet and slippery — walk carefully
- Do NOT use if you have respiratory conditions (asthma) without doctor's approval
- Shower after use to remove toxins released through sweating

1.4 Spa Emergency Response:
- If a guest faints or collapses during treatment:
  - Stop the treatment immediately
  - Place the guest in recovery position (on their side)
  - Call for medical assistance (dial emergency extension)
  - Keep the guest warm with towels
  - Monitor breathing until help arrives
- If a guest has an allergic reaction to a product:
  - Remove the product immediately with water
  - Apply cold compress if skin is irritated
  - Call for medical assistance
  - Document the product used and the reaction

2. GYM AND FITNESS CENTER SAFETY

2.1 General Gym Rules:
- Guests must be 16 or older to use the gym (under 18 with adult supervision)
- Wipe down equipment after use with provided sanitizing wipes
- Wear appropriate footwear (closed-toe athletic shoes)
- Use safety clips on treadmills and weight machines
- Do NOT drop free weights
- Ask staff for assistance if unfamiliar with equipment
- Keep the area around equipment clear

2.2 Gym Emergency Response:
- If a guest collapses or has a cardiac event:
  - Call 911 immediately
  - Use the AED located at the gym entrance
  - Begin CPR if trained (see Medical Emergency Protocol)
  - Do NOT move the person unless they are in danger
- If a guest is injured by equipment:
  - Stop the equipment immediately (hit emergency stop button)
  - Assess the injury — call for medical help if needed
  - Apply first aid (ice for sprains, pressure for cuts)
  - Document the incident, including the equipment involved

2.3 Common Gym Injuries:
- Muscle strains: apply RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation)
- Cuts from equipment: clean with antiseptic, apply bandage
- Fainting/dizziness: usually from dehydration or overexertion — rest, drink water
- Treadmill falls: clean any abrasions, check for sprains or fractures
- Weight drops: assess for broken bones, apply ice, seek medical attention

3. OUTDOOR ADVENTURE ACTIVITIES (RESORT-SPECIFIC)

3.1 Hiking and Nature Trails:
- Stay on marked trails at all times
- Inform the front desk of your hiking plans and expected return time
- Carry water, sunscreen, and a charged phone
- Wear appropriate footwear (closed-toe, non-slip)
- Watch for wildlife — do NOT approach or feed wild animals
- In case of snakebite:
  - Move away from the snake
  - Keep the bitten area below heart level
  - Do NOT try to suck out the venom
  - Do NOT apply a tourniquet
  - Call 911 and get to medical help as quickly as possible
  - Try to remember the snake's appearance for identification

3.2 Cycling and Mountain Biking:
- Helmets are MANDATORY
- Check brakes and tires before riding
- Stay on designated cycling paths
- Ride single file on roads
- Use hand signals for turning
- In case of accident: assess for injuries, do NOT remove the helmet, call for help

3.3 Zip-lining and Rope Courses:
- Follow ALL instructions from the activity guide
- Wear the provided safety harness and helmet at all times
- Do NOT modify or adjust equipment yourself
- Weight limits must be strictly observed
- Do NOT attempt the activity if you have heart conditions or are pregnant
- In case of equipment malfunction: remain still and calm, call for the guide

3.4 Horseback Riding:
- Helmets are MANDATORY
- Always mount and dismount on the left side
- Keep a safe distance from other horses
- Do NOT run or make sudden movements around horses
- In case of a fall:
  - Protect your head and curl into a ball
  - Move away from the horse as soon as possible
  - Check for injuries before standing
  - Report any fall to the guide immediately

3.5 Golf:
- Yell "FORE!" if your ball heads toward another person
- Wait until the group ahead is out of range before hitting
- Never swing a club near another person
- Lightning protocol: if you hear thunder or see lightning:
  - Stop play immediately
  - Do NOT shelter under trees
  - Go to the clubhouse or a lightning shelter
  - Avoid holding metal clubs
  - Resume play only after 30 minutes of no thunder/lightning

4. CHILDREN'S ACTIVITY PROGRAMS (KIDS' CLUB)

4.1 Safety Requirements:
- All children must be registered with parent/guardian contact information
- Allergies and medical conditions must be documented
- Staff-to-child ratio: minimum 1 adult for every 8 children
- Children may only be released to registered parents/guardians (ID verification)
- First aid kit available at all times
- Staff must have current first aid and CPR certifications

4.2 Activity Safety:
- Age-appropriate activities only
- Outdoor activities require sunscreen (applied by parents before drop-off)
- Water activities: additional lifeguard supervision required
- All craft supplies must be non-toxic
- Sharp objects (scissors, etc.) used only with direct staff supervision

4.3 Emergency Procedures:
- If a child is injured: administer first aid, contact parents immediately
- If a child goes missing: initiate the Missing Person Protocol immediately
  - Secure all exits
  - Begin systematic search of the premises
  - Contact security and management
  - Call parents
  - If not found within 15 minutes, call police
- If a child has an allergic reaction: administer EpiPen if authorized by parents, call 911

5. NIGHTTIME AND ENTERTAINMENT SAFETY

5.1 Evening Events and Shows:
- Emergency exits must be clearly marked and unobstructed
- Staff must know evacuation routes from event spaces
- Maximum capacity limits must be enforced
- Fire extinguishers accessible within 75 feet of any point

5.2 Nightclub/Bar Area:
- Staff trained in responsible alcohol service
- Intoxicated guests should be offered assistance to their room (escort, wheelchair)
- Do NOT allow intoxicated guests to swim, drive, or operate any equipment
- Keep pathways well-lit for guests returning to rooms after dark

5.3 Outdoor Evening Activities (Bonfires, BBQ):
- Fire pit must be supervised by staff at all times
- Keep fire extinguisher and water source within reach
- Maintain a 10-foot clear zone around any open flame
- Completely extinguish all fires before leaving the area
- Do NOT allow guests to add fuel to fires (staff only)

6. GENERAL RECREATIONAL SAFETY PRINCIPLES

- Always follow posted rules and signage
- If you feel unsafe about ANY activity, do NOT participate — inform staff
- Report any damaged or malfunctioning equipment immediately
- Do NOT operate recreational equipment while intoxicated
- Wear appropriate safety gear for all activities
- Stay hydrated and take breaks, especially in hot weather
- Inform staff of any pre-existing medical conditions before activities
- The resort is not liable for injuries resulting from violations of posted rules
- In case of ANY injury, report it to staff immediately — do NOT wait
- Emergency services can be reached by dialing 911 or the front desk (dial 0)
