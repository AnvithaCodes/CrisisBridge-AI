CRISISBRIDGE AI — SECURITY INCIDENT PROTOCOL
Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. TYPES OF SECURITY INCIDENTS

1.1 Suspicious Person/Activity:
- Unknown individual loitering in restricted areas
- Person attempting to access guest rooms without a key
- Individual photographing security equipment or restricted areas
- Person behaving erratically or aggressively

1.2 Theft:
- Guest room burglary
- Theft from common areas (lobby, pool, restaurant)
- Vehicle break-in (parking lot)
- Employee theft

1.3 Unauthorized Access:
- Entry into staff-only areas
- Trespassing on resort grounds
- Tailgating through secure doors

1.4 Assault or Violence:
- Physical altercation between guests
- Domestic disturbance in guest rooms
- Threats against staff or guests

1.5 Active Threat:
- Armed individual on property
- Bomb threat
- Hostage situation

2. RESPONSE PROCEDURES

2.1 Suspicious Person:
- Do NOT confront the individual directly
- Observe and note: appearance, clothing, direction of movement, any vehicle
- Report to hotel security immediately (dial security extension)
- Security will assess and respond
- If the person is in a guest area, politely ask "May I help you?" to determine if they are a guest
- If they cannot provide a room number or guest name, escort them to the lobby

2.2 Theft Response:
For Guests:
- Report the theft to the front desk immediately
- Do NOT touch or disturb the scene
- Make a list of stolen items with descriptions and approximate values
- Hotel staff will contact local police
- Request a copy of the police report for insurance purposes
- Check if the hotel safe was available and whether valuable items were secured

For Staff:
- Secure the area where the theft occurred
- Review CCTV footage if available
- Interview witnesses
- File a police report
- Document everything in the incident log

2.3 Unauthorized Access:
- Challenge unknown individuals in restricted areas: "Excuse me, can I help you?"
- If they claim to be a guest, verify with the front desk
- If they are not authorized, politely escort them out
- Report the incident to security
- Review and reinforce access control measures

2.4 Assault/Violence:
- Personal safety is the top priority — do NOT put yourself at risk
- Call security and police immediately
- If safe, try to de-escalate verbally: speak calmly, do not argue
- Clear other guests from the area
- If someone is injured, call for medical assistance
- Document the incident thoroughly
- Offer support to victims (first aid, private area, contact information for local support services)

2.5 Active Threat — RUN, HIDE, FIGHT:
RUN:
- If there is an accessible escape path, attempt to evacuate
- Leave belongings behind
- Help others escape if possible
- Prevent others from entering the danger zone
- Call 911 when safe

HIDE:
- If evacuation is not possible, find a place to hide
- Lock and barricade doors
- Turn off lights
- Silence phones
- Stay quiet and out of sight
- Hide behind large objects (desks, beds)

FIGHT:
- As a LAST resort, and only if your life is in immediate danger
- Act with physical aggression
- Use improvised weapons (fire extinguisher, chair, lamp)
- Commit to your actions — your life depends on it

3. BEACH AND POOL AREA SECURITY (RESORT-SPECIFIC)

3.1 Beach Security:
- Security patrols should cover beach areas every 30 minutes during operating hours
- Guest wristbands or key cards required for access to private beach areas
- Report unregistered individuals on private beach to security
- Secure any rental equipment (kayaks, paddleboards) when not in use
- Monitor for unauthorized vendors on resort beach

3.2 Pool Area Security:
- Pool area is for registered guests only — key card access required
- No glass containers in the pool area
- Security cameras should cover all pool entrances
- Theft of personal belongings at poolside: report immediately
- Intoxicated individuals must be asked to leave the pool area for safety

4. REPORTING AND DOCUMENTATION

4.1 Incident Report Must Include:
- Date, time, and exact location of the incident
- Names and room numbers of involved parties
- Description of what happened (factual, not opinion)
- Names of witnesses
- Actions taken by staff
- Any police involvement (officer names, badge numbers, report number)
- Photographs of any evidence or damage

4.2 Guest Communication:
- Inform affected guests of actions taken
- Provide police report numbers when available
- Offer room changes if the guest feels unsafe
- Maintain confidentiality — do not discuss incidents with uninvolved guests
- Follow up with affected guests within 24 hours

5. PREVENTION MEASURES

- All exterior doors should be locked after 10 PM (guest key card access only)
- CCTV cameras at all entrances, parking areas, and common spaces
- Adequate lighting in all parking areas, pathways, and building exteriors
- Staff training on recognizing suspicious behavior
- Regular security patrols of all floors and resort areas
- Guest room safes available in every room
- "Do Not Disturb" signs should not be honored beyond 24 hours without a welfare check
- Buddy system for staff working late shifts
