CRISISBRIDGE AI — CRISIS COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL
Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. CENTRALIZED REPORTING & "THE SINGLE SOURCE OF TRUTH"

1.1 The CrisisBridge Dashboard:
- During any emergency, the CrisisBridge Incident Dashboard is the SINGLE SOURCE OF TRUTH for all staff.
- Do NOT rely on verbal rumors or fragmented text chains.
- All staff must check the dashboard for real-time status updates, incident locations, and assigned responders.
- The dashboard syncs across all devices to eliminate fragmented communication.

1.2 Reporting an Incident (Guests & Staff):
- Use the CrisisBridge "Panic Button" or report feature in the app.
- This immediately logs the incident type, location, and timestamp into the central system.
- If a guest reports an emergency verbally to a staff member, the staff member MUST log it into the CrisisBridge system immediately to trigger the coordinated response.

2. COMMUNICATION WITH EMERGENCY SERVICES (911)

2.1 Who Calls 911?
- ANY staff member or guest who witnesses a life-threatening emergency (fire, severe medical, active threat) should call 911 immediately.
- Do NOT wait for management approval to call 911 if lives are at risk.
- Once 911 is called, notify the Front Desk (dial 0) and log it in the CrisisBridge system.

2.2 What to Tell 911 Dispatch:
- Your name and title.
- The hotel/resort name and exact street address.
- The EXACT location of the emergency (Floor 4, Room 412, or "Main Pool Deck").
- The nature of the emergency and number of people involved.
- Stay on the line until the dispatcher tells you to hang up.

3. STAFF-TO-STAFF COMMUNICATION

3.1 The Incident Commander:
- The General Manager (or highest-ranking manager on duty) becomes the Incident Commander (IC).
- The IC operates from the Front Desk or a safe command post.
- The IC is responsible for assigning tasks via the CrisisBridge dashboard.

3.2 Two-Way Radios (Walkie-Talkies):
- Keep radio communication BRIEF and PROFESSIONAL.
- Use plain language (no confusing codes like "Code Red" — say "Fire in Room 302").
- Do not transmit sensitive guest information (names, room numbers) over public radio channels unless necessary for immediate safety. Use the CrisisBridge app for secure data sharing.

4. GUEST COMMUNICATION

4.1 Guiding Principles:
- Be CALM, CLEAR, and CONFIDENT.
- Provide factual information, not speculation.
- Do NOT say "Everything is fine" if it is not. Instead, say "We are handling the situation, please follow my instructions."

4.2 Automated Mass Broadcasts:
- The Incident Commander can trigger mass alerts via the CrisisBridge system.
- Examples of clear broadcasts:
  - "Attention guests: A fire has been reported on Floor 3. All guests on Floors 2, 3, and 4 must evacuate via the stairs immediately."
  - "Attention guests: We are sheltering in place due to severe weather. Please remain in your rooms away from windows."

4.3 Interacting with Distressed Guests:
- Empathy first: "I understand you are scared, I am here to help you."
- Clear directions: "I need you to leave your bags and walk to the exit with me now."
- If guests refuse to evacuate, do not argue or physically force them. Note their room number and report it to the Incident Commander/Fire Department.

5. MEDIA AND EXTERNAL COMMUNICATION

- Staff members are NOT authorized to speak to the media.
- If approached by a reporter, reply: "Our priority right now is the safety of our guests and staff. Our designated spokesperson will provide a statement shortly."
- Do NOT post photos, videos, or status updates about the ongoing emergency on personal social media accounts.
- The General Manager or corporate PR team will handle all external statements.
