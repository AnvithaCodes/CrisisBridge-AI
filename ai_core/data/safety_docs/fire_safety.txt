Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS WHEN FIRE IS DETECTED

1.1 For Guests:
- If you see fire or smoke, immediately alert others in the area by shouting "FIRE!"
- Do NOT attempt to fight a large fire yourself
- Feel the door before opening — if it is hot, DO NOT open it
- If the door is cool, open it slowly and check for smoke in the hallway
- Stay low to the ground if there is smoke (crawl if necessary)
- Proceed to the nearest emergency exit — DO NOT use elevators
- If trapped in your room, seal the door gaps with wet towels
- Call the front desk (dial 0) or emergency services (dial 911)
- Go to the designated assembly point in the parking lot or main lawn area

1.2 For Staff:
- Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station
- Call the fire department immediately (do not assume someone else has called)
- Begin evacuating guests from the affected floor
- Use a buddy system — never enter a fire area alone
- Report to the Incident Commander at the front lobby
- Account for all guests using the guest registry

2. FIRE TYPES AND APPROPRIATE RESPONSES

2.1 Hotel Room Fire:
- Most common cause: unattended candles, smoking materials, electrical faults
- Small fires (wastebasket, curtain): Use the in-room fire extinguisher (PASS technique)
  P — Pull the pin
  A — Aim at the base of the fire
  S — Squeeze the handle
  S — Sweep side to side
- If the fire grows beyond a wastebasket size, evacuate immediately
- Close the door behind you to contain the fire

2.2 Restaurant/Kitchen Fire:
- Grease fires: NEVER use water — use a Class K fire extinguisher or fire blanket
- Turn off the heat source immediately
- If the fire is in a deep fryer, cover with a metal lid
- Activate the kitchen hood suppression system
- Evacuate the restaurant and close doors

2.3 Resort Cabin/Villa Fire:
- Same procedures as hotel room fires
- Additional concern: cabins may be more isolated — call for help immediately
- Check surrounding cabins and alert neighbors
- Clear pathways to allow fire truck access

2.4 Outdoor BBQ/Fire Pit Area:
- Keep fire extinguisher within 10 feet of any open flame cooking area
- Never leave a fire pit or BBQ grill unattended
- In case of fire spread to vegetation, call the fire department immediately
- Evacuate the outdoor area and move guests indoors or to a safe distance (minimum 100 feet)

3. EVACUATION ROUTES

3.1 Main Building:
- Primary exits: Front lobby doors, rear emergency exits
- Secondary exits: Side stairwell doors on each floor
- All stairwells lead to ground floor exits
- Emergency lighting activates automatically during power failure
- Follow the illuminated exit signs

3.2 Resort Areas:
- Pool area: Exit through the pool gate to the main parking lot
- Spa/Wellness center: Exit through the spa entrance to the garden path
- Beach area: Move away from buildings to the open beach
- Golf course/activity areas: Move to the nearest road or parking area

4. FIRE PREVENTION MEASURES

- No smoking in guest rooms (designated smoking areas only)
- Do not overload electrical outlets
- Report any frayed wires or malfunctioning equipment to maintenance immediately
- Keep hallways and exits clear of obstructions at all times
- Fire doors must remain closed at all times — do not prop them open
- All staff must complete annual fire safety training

5. ASSEMBLY POINTS

- Main Assembly Point: Front parking lot (by the flagpole/main entrance)
- Secondary Assembly Point: Rear garden/lawn area
- Resort Activity Assembly Point: Main pool deck
- Beach Assembly Point: Lifeguard station area
- Staff must conduct a headcount at the assembly point
- Do NOT re-enter the building until the "All Clear" is given by the fire department

6. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

6.1 Guests with Disabilities:
- Staff must assist guests with mobility impairments to evacuate
- Use evacuation chairs available on each floor near the stairwell
- Visually impaired guests: staff must provide verbal guidance and physical assistance
- Hearing impaired guests: ensure visual alarm strobes are functioning

6.2 Children and Families:
- Parents should account for all children immediately
- Kid's club staff are responsible for evacuating all children in their care
- Infants: carry them, do not use strollers in stairwells

6.3 Late Night/Early Morning:
- Night staff must be prepared to wake and evacuate sleeping guests
- Use loud knocking and verbal commands: "FIRE! PLEASE EVACUATE!"
- Check every room on the affected floor
