Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. EARTHQUAKE

1.1 During the Earthquake:
- DROP to the ground
- Take COVER under a sturdy desk, table, or doorframe
- HOLD ON until the shaking stops
- Stay AWAY from windows, mirrors, heavy furniture, and hanging objects
- If in bed, stay there — protect your head with a pillow
- If outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines
- If near the ocean, move to higher ground immediately after shaking stops (tsunami risk)

1.2 After the Earthquake:
- Check yourself and others for injuries
- Expect aftershocks — they can be nearly as strong as the initial quake
- Do NOT use elevators
- Exit the building using stairs if safe to do so
- If you smell gas, leave immediately and report it
- Do NOT turn on light switches (spark risk if gas is present)
- Check for structural damage before moving through hallways
- Proceed to the assembly point

1.3 Staff Response:
- Activate earthquake response plan
- Check all floors for injured or trapped guests
- Shut off gas lines if trained to do so
- Check water mains and electrical systems for damage
- Do NOT re-enter severely damaged buildings
- Account for all guests at assembly points
- Contact emergency services for injuries and structural assessment

2. FLOOD

2.1 Before Flooding (Warning Received):
- Monitor local weather and flood warnings continuously
- Move critical equipment and valuables to upper floors
- Prepare sandbags for ground-level entry points
- Pre-position emergency supplies (water, food, first aid, flashlights)
- Consider pre-emptive evacuation of ground floor rooms
- Fuel all emergency vehicles and generators
- Notify all guests of the flood risk and preparation measures

2.2 During Flooding:
- Do NOT walk, swim, or drive through flood waters
- 6 inches of moving water can knock you down
- 12 inches of moving water can carry away a vehicle
- Move to upper floors if water is rising
- Turn off electrical breakers for flooded areas
- Avoid contact with flood water (it may be contaminated)
- Stay away from downed power lines
- Call 911 if anyone is in immediate danger

2.3 After Flooding:
- Do NOT return to flooded areas until authorities say it is safe
- Watch for weakened floors, walls, and foundations
- Clean and disinfect everything that got wet
- Discard any food that came in contact with flood water
- Document all damage with photographs for insurance
- Check for mold growth in the days following the flood

3. SEVERE STORM (THUNDERSTORM, HURRICANE, TYPHOON)

3.1 Before the Storm:
- Secure all outdoor furniture, equipment, and decorations
- Close and lock all windows and doors
- Draw curtains and blinds (protection from breaking glass)
- Charge all phones and emergency devices
- Fill bathtubs with water (backup supply in case water is cut off)
- Stock each floor with emergency supplies
- Brief all staff on storm response procedures
- Move guests from beachfront cabins/villas to the main building

3.2 During the Storm:
- Stay indoors, away from windows
- Proceed to interior rooms on lower floors (not basement if flooding is possible)
- If lightning: stay away from water, plumbing, and electrical equipment
- Do NOT use corded phones during a thunderstorm
- If power is lost, use flashlights (not candles)
- Monitor weather updates via battery-powered radio or phone
- Do NOT go outside during the eye of a hurricane — the storm will resume

3.3 After the Storm:
- Wait for the official "All Clear" before going outside
- Watch for downed power lines, broken glass, and debris
- Check for gas leaks (smell, hissing sounds)
- Report any damage to the front desk or management
- Do NOT use tap water until confirmed safe
- Stay away from damaged structures

4. TSUNAMI

4.1 Warning Signs:
- Strong earthquake felt near the coast
- Unusual recession of water from the shoreline
- Official tsunami warning from authorities
- Loud roaring sound from the ocean

4.2 Immediate Actions:
- Move to HIGH GROUND immediately — at least 100 feet above sea level
- Move INLAND — at least 2 miles from the coast
- Do NOT stay to watch — tsunami waves travel at 500+ mph
- Do NOT return to low areas after the first wave — more waves will follow
- If on the beach, RUN to the highest point you can reach
- If in a building, go to the highest floor and move to the inland side

4.3 Staff Response:
- Sound the tsunami alarm immediately
- Begin evacuating all beach, pool, and ground-floor areas
- Direct guests to the highest floors of reinforced concrete buildings
- If time allows, drive to higher ground
- Account for all guests after reaching safety
- Do NOT return until official "All Clear" from authorities

5. TORNADO

5.1 During a Tornado Warning:
- Move immediately to the lowest floor of the building
- Go to an interior room, hallway, or bathroom (away from windows)
- Stay away from windows, doors, and outside walls
- Get under a sturdy piece of furniture
- Cover your head and neck with your arms or a pillow
- If outdoors, lie flat in a ditch or low area — cover your head
- Do NOT stay in a vehicle or mobile structure

5.2 After a Tornado:
- Check for injuries and provide first aid
- Do NOT enter damaged buildings
- Watch for broken glass, nails, and sharp debris
- Report gas leaks, downed power lines, and structural damage
- Account for all guests

6. EXTREME HEAT

6.1 Heat Advisory Response:
- Ensure all air conditioning is functioning
- Provide free water at all public areas (lobby, pool, beach)
- Reduce outdoor activities during peak heat (11 AM - 4 PM)
- Monitor elderly guests and those with medical conditions
- Provide shaded areas at all outdoor activity points
- Watch for signs of heat exhaustion and heatstroke (see Medical Emergency Protocol)

6.2 For Guests:
- Stay hydrated — drink water regularly
- Wear light, loose-fitting clothing
- Use sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher, reapply every 2 hours)
- Avoid prolonged sun exposure
- Take breaks in air-conditioned spaces
- Avoid alcohol during extreme heat (it dehydrates)
- If you feel dizzy, nauseous, or confused, move to a cool area immediately and tell staff

7. COASTAL STORM SURGE (RESORT-SPECIFIC)

- Storm surge can raise water levels 15+ feet above normal
- Evacuate all beach-level and ground-floor areas when storm surge is predicted
- Move all guests to the 3rd floor or higher
- Secure all watercraft and beach equipment
- Do NOT attempt to surf, swim, or walk on the beach during storm surge conditions
- After the surge recedes, check for structural damage before allowing access

8. GENERAL NATURAL DISASTER PREPAREDNESS

8.1 Emergency Kit Locations:
- Every floor has an emergency kit containing:
  - First aid supplies
  - Flashlights and extra batteries
  - Battery-powered radio
  - Bottled water
  - Non-perishable snacks
  - Emergency blankets
  - Whistle (for signaling)

8.2 Communication Plan:
- Staff will communicate via walkie-talkies if phones are down
- PA system on backup generator power
- Guest notification via door-to-door visits if electronic systems fail
- Emergency information posted in the lobby

8.3 Post-Disaster:
- Do NOT return to your room until cleared by management
- Follow all instructions from emergency services
- Hotel will arrange alternative accommodation if the property is uninhabitable
- Keep all receipts for insurance claims
