CRISISBRIDGE AI — WATER SAFETY PROTOCOL
Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. SWIMMING POOL SAFETY

1.1 General Pool Rules:
- No running on the pool deck (wet surfaces are extremely slippery)
- No diving in shallow areas (minimum depth for diving: 9 feet / 2.7 meters)
- No glass containers in the pool area
- Children under 12 must be accompanied by an adult at all times
- No swimming while intoxicated
- Shower before entering the pool
- Report any unsafe conditions to pool staff immediately

1.2 Pool Emergencies:
- If you see someone in distress in the water, alert the lifeguard IMMEDIATELY
- Do NOT enter the water to rescue someone unless you are a trained lifeguard
- Throw a flotation device (life ring, pool noodle) to the person
- Use the reaching method: extend a pole, towel, or your arm from a safe position
- If no lifeguard is present, call 911 and begin rescue efforts only if trained

1.3 Lifeguard Coverage:
- Pool is staffed with lifeguards from 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily
- After-hours swimming is at your own risk (posted signage)
- Lifeguards are trained in CPR, first aid, and water rescue
- AED is located at the lifeguard station
- Lifeguards have authority to close the pool for safety reasons

1.4 Drowning Prevention:
- Drowning is SILENT — victims rarely call for help or wave their arms
- Signs of drowning:
  - Head tilted back, mouth at water level
  - Eyes glassy and unfocused
  - Vertical body position, not kicking
  - Attempting to swim but not making progress
  - Hyperventilating or gasping
- If someone is missing, CHECK THE POOL FIRST

2. CHILDREN'S POOL AND WATER PLAY AREAS

- Children's pool maximum depth: 18 inches
- Constant adult supervision required — this is NOT a babysitting area
- Water play structures: children must follow posted height requirements
- Infants and toddlers: swim diapers required
- No roughhousing or pushing near water features
- Parents should teach children to always ask before entering the water

3. BEACH SAFETY (RESORT-SPECIFIC)

3.1 Beach Rules:
- Swim only in designated areas marked by flags/buoys
- Red flag: NO swimming — dangerous conditions
- Yellow flag: Caution — moderate conditions, swim with care
- Green flag: Safe — calm conditions
- Do NOT swim alone — always use the buddy system
- Do NOT swim at night
- Do NOT swim near piers, jetties, or rock formations
- Notify lifeguards if you plan to use personal watercraft

3.2 Rip Currents:
- Rip currents are the #1 beach hazard
- Signs of a rip current:
  - A channel of churning, choppy water
  - A line of foam, seaweed, or debris moving seaward
  - A break in the incoming wave pattern
  - Discolored water
- If caught in a rip current:
  - Do NOT panic
  - Do NOT try to swim directly back to shore (you will exhaust yourself)
  - Swim PARALLEL to the shore until you are out of the current
  - Then swim diagonally back to shore
  - If you cannot swim out, float or tread water and wave for help
  - Call for help — shout and wave one arm

3.3 Marine Hazards:
- Jellyfish stings:
  - Exit the water
  - Rinse with seawater (NOT fresh water)
  - Remove tentacles with tweezers or a credit card edge (NOT bare hands)
  - Apply vinegar or baking soda paste
  - Seek medical attention for severe reactions (difficulty breathing, chest pain)
- Sea urchins: do NOT step on them, wear water shoes in rocky areas
- Sharks: if spotted, exit the water calmly, alert lifeguards
- Coral: do NOT touch or stand on coral (injury risk and environmental damage)

4. WATER SPORTS SAFETY

4.1 Kayaking and Canoeing:
- Wear a life jacket AT ALL TIMES
- Check weather conditions before departing
- Stay within designated water sports zones
- Do NOT exceed the vessel's weight capacity
- If capsized: stay with the vessel, it will float
- Signal for help if unable to return to shore

4.2 Paddleboarding (SUP):
- Wear a life jacket or have one attached to the board
- Use a leash connecting you to the board
- Start in calm, shallow water if you are a beginner
- Check weather and wind conditions before heading out
- Avoid areas with boat traffic

4.3 Snorkeling:
- Never snorkel alone — always use the buddy system
- Check equipment before entering the water (mask seal, snorkel clear)
- Stay aware of your surroundings and distance from shore
- Do NOT touch or disturb marine life
- If feeling tired, float on your back and rest
- Use a snorkel vest or flotation device if you are not a strong swimmer

4.4 Jet Ski / Personal Watercraft:
- Minimum age: 16 years (with valid ID)
- Briefing session required before operation
- Wear a life jacket at all times
- Stay within the designated operating area
- Maintain safe distance from swimmers, boats, and shore (minimum 200 feet)
- Maximum 2 persons per jet ski
- Do NOT operate under the influence of alcohol

5. WATER SLIDE SAFETY

- Follow all posted height and weight requirements
- Wait for the attendant's signal before going down
- Go feet first, lying on your back
- Do NOT stand up, kneel, or go headfirst
- Do NOT stop or slow down in the slide
- Clear the landing area immediately after arriving
- Report any injury or unsafe condition to the attendant

6. HOT TUB / JACUZZI SAFETY

- Maximum recommended time: 15 minutes
- Do NOT use if water temperature exceeds 104°F (40°C)
- Do NOT use if pregnant (consult your doctor)
- Do NOT use while intoxicated
- Do NOT submerge your head
- Children under 5 should NOT use hot tubs
- Exit immediately if you feel dizzy, nauseous, or overheated
- Shower before and after use
- Stay hydrated — drink water before and after

7. DROWNING RESPONSE PROCEDURE

Step 1: Recognize the emergency
- Drowning is SILENT — look for the signs listed in Section 1.4

Step 2: Alert help
- Shout for the lifeguard
- Call 911 or have someone call

Step 3: Reach or throw (do NOT enter the water unless trained)
- Throw a life ring, pool noodle, or rope
- Extend a pole, towel, or shepherd's crook

Step 4: If the person is removed from the water:
- Check for breathing
- If not breathing, begin CPR immediately (see Medical Emergency Protocol)
- Use an AED if available
- Continue until emergency services arrive

Step 5: After rescue:
- ALL near-drowning victims MUST go to the hospital
- Secondary drowning can occur 1-24 hours after the incident
- Symptoms: coughing, chest pain, difficulty breathing, extreme fatigue
