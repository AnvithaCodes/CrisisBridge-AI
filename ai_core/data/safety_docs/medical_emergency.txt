Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. GENERAL MEDICAL EMERGENCY RESPONSE

1.1 First Response Steps:
- Stay calm and assess the situation
- Call emergency services immediately (dial 911 or local emergency number)
- Notify the front desk (dial 0) — they will dispatch trained staff
- Do NOT move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger
- Stay with the patient until medical help arrives
- If trained, begin first aid procedures

1.2 Information to Provide to Emergency Services:
- Your name and location (hotel name, room number, floor, or specific resort area)
- Nature of the emergency (what happened)
- Number of people affected
- Current condition of the patient(s)
- Any first aid already administered

2. CPR PROCEDURE (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

2.1 For Adults:
- Check for responsiveness — tap shoulders and shout "Are you okay?"
- If unresponsive, call 911 and get an AED (Automated External Defibrillator)
- Place the person on their back on a firm, flat surface
- Begin chest compressions:
  - Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest
  - Place the other hand on top, interlace fingers
  - Push hard and fast: 2 inches deep, rate of 100-120 compressions per minute
  - Allow full chest recoil between compressions
- After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths:
  - Tilt the head back, lift the chin
  - Pinch the nose, seal your mouth over theirs
  - Blow for about 1 second, watch for chest rise
- Continue 30:2 ratio until help arrives or the person recovers
- If you are not trained in rescue breaths, perform compression-only CPR

2.2 For Children (ages 1-8):
- Use one hand for compressions
- Push about 1.5 inches deep
- Same 30:2 ratio

2.3 For Infants (under 1 year):
- Use two fingers for compressions on the breastbone
- Push about 1.5 inches deep
- Cover both mouth and nose for rescue breaths
- Use gentle puffs of air

3. AED (Automated External Defibrillator) USE

- AED locations in the hotel:
  - Front lobby (near reception desk)
  - Each floor (near the elevator lobby)
  - Pool area (lifeguard station)
  - Gym/fitness center
  - Spa reception
  - Restaurant area
- How to use:
  1. Turn on the AED — it will give voice instructions
  2. Attach the pads to the patient's bare chest as shown in the diagram
  3. Ensure no one is touching the patient
  4. Press the "Analyze" button
  5. If a shock is advised, press the "Shock" button
  6. Resume CPR immediately after the shock
  7. Continue following AED instructions until paramedics arrive

4. CHOKING RESPONSE

4.1 For Adults and Children (over 1 year):
- Ask "Are you choking? Can you speak?"
- If they cannot speak, cough, or breathe:
  - Stand behind the person
  - Make a fist with one hand, place it above the navel
  - Grasp the fist with the other hand
  - Perform quick upward thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)
  - Repeat until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious
- If the person becomes unconscious:
  - Lower them to the ground
  - Call 911
  - Begin CPR — check the mouth for the object before giving breaths

4.2 For Infants (under 1 year):
- Hold the infant face-down on your forearm
- Support the head and neck
- Give 5 back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand
- Turn the infant face-up
- Give 5 chest thrusts (like CPR compressions)
- Repeat until the object is expelled

5. ALLERGIC REACTIONS AND ANAPHYLAXIS

- Signs: hives, swelling (face, lips, throat), difficulty breathing, rapid pulse, dizziness
- If the person has an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen):
  - Help them use it — inject into the outer thigh
  - Can be used through clothing
  - Hold in place for 10 seconds
- Call 911 immediately — even after using an EpiPen
- Have the person lie down with legs elevated (unless they have breathing difficulty)
- If breathing is difficult, let them sit upright
- Be prepared to perform CPR if they become unresponsive
- Note: Common allergens in hotel/resort settings: shellfish, nuts, bee stings, latex

6. HEATSTROKE AND HEAT EXHAUSTION (RESORT-SPECIFIC)

6.1 Heat Exhaustion:
- Signs: heavy sweating, weakness, cold/pale/clammy skin, nausea, dizziness
- Move the person to a cool, air-conditioned area
- Lay them down and elevate their legs
- Apply cool, wet cloths to the body
- Give sips of water (not gulps)
- If symptoms worsen or last longer than 1 hour, call 911

6.2 Heatstroke (MEDICAL EMERGENCY):
- Signs: body temperature above 103°F, hot/red/dry skin, rapid pulse, confusion, unconsciousness
- Call 911 IMMEDIATELY — this is life-threatening
- Move to a cool area
- Cool the body rapidly: cold water immersion, ice packs to neck/armpits/groin
- Do NOT give fluids if the person is unconscious
- Fan the person while wetting their skin

7. DROWNING AND NEAR-DROWNING (see also Water Safety Protocol)

- Remove the person from the water immediately
- Check for breathing and pulse
- If not breathing, begin CPR immediately
- Call 911
- Even if the person recovers, they MUST be evaluated at a hospital
  (secondary drowning can occur hours later)
- Keep the person warm with blankets or towels

8. FRACTURES AND SPRAINS

- Do NOT move the injured limb
- Apply ice wrapped in a cloth (never directly on skin)
- Elevate the injured area if possible
- Immobilize the area with a splint or sling if trained
- Call for medical assistance
- Common resort injury locations: hiking trails, water slides, gym, wet pool decks

9. BURNS

9.1 Minor Burns (small area, no blistering):
- Cool the burn under cool running water for at least 10 minutes
- Do NOT use ice, butter, or ointments
- Cover with a sterile, non-stick bandage
- Take over-the-counter pain medication if needed

9.2 Severe Burns (large area, blistering, charred skin):
- Call 911 immediately
- Do NOT remove clothing stuck to the burn
- Do NOT apply water to large severe burns (risk of hypothermia)
- Cover loosely with a clean, dry cloth
- Watch for signs of shock

10. WHEN TO CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES

Always call 911 for:
- Unconsciousness
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain
- Severe bleeding that won't stop
- Suspected stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
- Seizures
- Severe allergic reaction
- Suspected spinal injury
- Any situation where you are unsure — it is always better to call
