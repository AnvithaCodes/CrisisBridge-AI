CRISISBRIDGE AI — EVACUATION PROCEDURES
Hotel & Resort Emergency Guidelines
=========================================

1. WHEN TO EVACUATE

Evacuation is required when:
- Fire alarm is activated
- Fire or smoke is detected
- Gas leak is confirmed
- Structural damage (earthquake, explosion)
- Flood waters entering the building
- Bomb threat with credible indicators
- Any order from emergency services or hotel management

2. EVACUATION PROCEDURE — HOTEL BUILDING

2.1 For Guests:
Step 1: When you hear the fire alarm or evacuation announcement:
- Stop what you are doing immediately
- Take your room key card (for identification purposes)
- Leave all other belongings behind
- Put on shoes if readily available

Step 2: Exit your room:
- Feel the door before opening — if hot, do NOT open it (see "If Trapped" below)
- If the door is cool, open slowly and check the hallway
- Close your room door behind you (this slows fire spread)

Step 3: Proceed to the nearest exit:
- Walk, do NOT run
- Stay low if there is smoke
- Use the STAIRS — NEVER use elevators during an evacuation
- Follow the illuminated exit signs and floor markings
- Hold the handrail in stairwells

Step 4: Exit the building:
- Go directly to the designated assembly point
- Do NOT stop in the lobby
- Do NOT re-enter the building for any reason

Step 5: At the assembly point:
- Report to the staff member conducting the headcount
- Provide your name and room number
- Remain at the assembly point until the "All Clear" is given

2.2 If Trapped in Your Room:
- Call 911 and the front desk — tell them your exact room number
- Seal the door with wet towels or sheets to block smoke
- Move to the window — open it slightly for fresh air if needed
- Signal for help: hang a sheet from the window, use a flashlight, or call out
- Do NOT jump from upper floors
- Stay low — air is cleaner near the floor
- Wait for rescue

3. EVACUATION PROCEDURE — RESORT AREAS

3.1 Pool and Water Park Area:
- Lifeguards will blow continuous whistle blasts
- Exit the pool/water immediately
- Do NOT return to lockers for belongings
- Proceed directly to the Pool Assembly Point (main parking lot)
- Parents: account for all children immediately

3.2 Beach Area:
- Beach attendants will announce evacuation
- Move away from the water
- Proceed to the Beach Assembly Point (lifeguard station area or beach entrance)
- If the threat is from the ocean (tsunami warning), move to higher ground IMMEDIATELY
- Move at least 100 feet inland and 100 feet above sea level if possible

3.3 Spa and Wellness Center:
- Staff will announce evacuation
- Guests in treatment rooms will be assisted by their therapist
- Guests in saunas, steam rooms, or jacuzzis: exit immediately
- Grab a robe or towel for coverage
- Exit through the spa entrance to the Garden Assembly Point

3.4 Restaurant and Dining Areas:
- Staff will announce evacuation
- Leave all food, drinks, and personal items
- Exit through the nearest restaurant exit
- Kitchen staff: turn off all cooking equipment before evacuating
- Proceed to the nearest assembly point

3.5 Conference and Event Rooms:
- Event staff/coordinator will direct evacuation
- Do NOT gather equipment or materials
- Exit through the nearest marked exit
- Use alternative exits if primary exits are blocked

3.6 Outdoor Activity Areas (Golf, Tennis, Hiking):
- Activity supervisors will announce evacuation
- Abandon equipment
- Proceed to the nearest road or parking area
- If on hiking trails, return to the trailhead or nearest access point

4. ASSEMBLY POINTS

Main Assembly Points:
1. FRONT PARKING LOT — Primary (near main entrance/flagpole)
2. REAR LAWN AREA — Secondary (behind the main building)
3. POOL DECK — For pool/water park evacuees
4. BEACH ENTRANCE — For beach area evacuees
5. GARDEN PATH — For spa/wellness center evacuees

At the Assembly Point:
- Staff will conduct a headcount using the guest registry
- Missing persons will be reported to emergency services immediately
- Do NOT leave the assembly point without informing staff
- Medical aid stations will be set up at each assembly point if needed

5. SPECIAL EVACUATION PROCEDURES

5.1 Guests with Mobility Impairments:
- Evacuate to the nearest stairwell landing (Area of Rescue Assistance)
- Call 911 and inform them of the exact stairwell location
- Staff with evacuation chair training will assist
- NEVER use elevators unless specifically authorized by the fire department
- Trained staff should remain with the guest until firefighters arrive

5.2 Guests with Visual Impairments:
- Staff should announce themselves and offer assistance
- Guide the guest by allowing them to hold your arm
- Describe the route verbally as you walk
- Inform them of stairs, turns, and obstacles

5.3 Guests with Hearing Impairments:
- Visual alarm strobes will flash in rooms and hallways
- Staff should make eye contact and use gestures to communicate urgency
- Write notes if necessary: "FIRE — FOLLOW ME TO EXIT"
- Ensure visual notification devices are functioning in assigned rooms

5.4 Children in Kids' Club:
- Kids' club staff are responsible for ALL children in their care
- Use the buddy system — pair children together
- Take the attendance register
- Evacuate to the designated Kids' Club Assembly Point
- Contact parents at the assembly point using the registration information

5.5 Pets:
- Guests with pets should take them if possible
- Use a leash or carrier
- If the pet cannot be quickly retrieved, evacuate without it
- Inform staff that a pet is in the room

6. EVACUATION MAPS

- Evacuation route maps are posted:
  - On the back of every guest room door
  - In every hallway (near elevators and stairwells)
  - In all common areas (lobby, restaurant, gym, spa)
  - At pool and beach area entrances
- Maps show: your current location, nearest exits, stairwell locations, assembly points
- Familiarize yourself with the map upon check-in

7. COMMUNICATION DURING EVACUATION

- PA system announcements will provide instructions
- Staff with megaphones will be at key locations
- If the PA system fails, staff will go door-to-door
- Updates will be communicated at assembly points
- Guest communication app (if available) will send push notifications

8. AFTER THE EVACUATION

- Remain at the assembly point until the official "All Clear"
- The "All Clear" will be announced via PA system and by staff at assembly points
- Re-entry will be orderly, floor by floor
- If the building is deemed unsafe, alternative accommodation will be arranged
- Hotel management will communicate next steps
